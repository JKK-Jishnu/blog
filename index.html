About Me

I prefer to designate myself as an Engineer, although in my youth, I yearned to become a Scientist. A Scientist whose creations dazzled the world. During my formative years, I was an avid bibliophile. The unfolding of my existence took on a captivating turn when I successfully secured admission to a small college, fighting tooth and nail through a pre-university merit system. It was there that I had the opportunity to mingle with individuals from diverse strata of society and learned to differentiate between excellent and subpar resources. Despite its apparent simplicity, once you are able to identify reliable resources, life takes on a new meaning. If my recollection serves me correctly, Jio was launched during my third year of college. To borrow a phrase from Naval Ravikant, we live in a world with infinite leverage, and knowledge started exploding around me from that point forward. Following the completion of my studies, I worked as a service engineer in GCC for two years, primarily engaged in travelling and interacting with a wide range of people. I refer to this period as my "exposure" phase. Subsequently, I left my job and returned to India, travelling to several locations before ultimately settling in Bengaluru, the Mecca of Indian engineering, which boasts excellent weather, particularly for those hailing from this subcontinent.
